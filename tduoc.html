The Digital Utility of Conflict

is a work in progress.

Info

January 2021

Project funded by the Gould Center for Humanistic Studies

The Digital Utility of Conflict aims to break down the logistics of weaponry, combat design and systems from 21st century video games across a variety of genres, featuring commentary breaking down objectives, justifications, and the efficiency of conflict. The project will also include an analysis of user experience and user design of the games themselves, and be presented in full as an interactive, multimedia website with accompanying podcasts.


Pictured: the Service Weapon from Control (2019).

Project background & proposal

Over the 2020 winter break, I was selected for funding from the Gould Center for Humanistic Studies for a passion project of my choice.


I had wanted to spend some time looking at video games and analyzing differences in interaction design across genres for a while, and this opportunity let me team up with a friend to create a more holistic cross-examination of what drives players to actually play games, and the philosophy (if any) behind the combat in them.


We will be examining fictional narratives, game design literature, scientific capabilities, and motivations for conflict. The Digital Utility of Conflict treks through a series of art pieces, analysis and personal reflections — curating our own framework of digital conflict in the modern world. The website will present a variety of individual video games, ranging from multiplayer RPGs to rogue-like dungeon crawlers, with in-depth case studies.


We have also produced approximately 30-minute podcasts each week to discuss our findings and experiences over the course of the project in a more informal setting.
