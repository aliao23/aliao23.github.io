CaCO3

Magazine design and branding.

Info

2017

Client — CaCO3 magazine

CaCO3 is a popular science print magazine for younger readers between the ages of 11-18 that aims to present scientific concepts and discoveries in a modern, accessible, and visually stimulating format. The magazine had previously been running for around 3 years, and needed a major overhaul in identity, structure, and design.

Logo design (There's antimony, arsenic...)

I worked closely with the editor-in-chief to brainstorm concepts for a new identity and logo that would emphasize clarity while maintaining the versatility of the content.
We wanted something that could be instantly recognizable, so we used the periodic table — a key scientific point of reference — as inspiration.
The biggest issue we faced was working around the subscript of the O3, which looked out of place when the individual parts of the chemical formula (the Ca and the CO3) were placed linearly. We ultimately decided to merge both, keeping the square form of an individual entry in the periodic table but splitting the compound components into two separate lines. The end result is minimalistic and easily reproducible and formattable, debuting on the cover of the edition for A/W 2017.


Editorial design

I wasn’t very familiar with the pop-sci genre, so I began by looking at other contemporary pop-sci magazines — starting with the New Scientist and the National Geographic.
Print magazines tend to center around feature spreads with bright colors and graphics, but often sacrifice legibility and accessibility for visual style. I wanted to maintain the wide color palette in previous editions of CaCO3, but approach it from a more minimalist perspective — one which focused more on emphasizing the text content.
